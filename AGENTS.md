# AGENTS.md

## Purpose

This document guides AI collaborators working on **The House Without Walls**.

This is a philosophical science fiction novel set in the continuity of *What It Feels Like to Be You* and *What It Feels Like to Be Us*. The novel explores shared dreaming as an unintended side effect of black market emote use, leading to the emergence of a shifting dream-space called **The House Without Walls**.

The house is not merely a place. It is a process. It is an evolving shared interior assembled from human memory, emotional residue, trauma, longing, and connection.

The novel's tone, themes, and worldview matter more than plot cleverness.

## High-Level Creative Directives

### 1. Protect the tone

The tone should remain:

- eerie
- intimate
- melancholy
- psychologically uncanny
- emotionally raw
- philosophically curious

Avoid turning this into:

- generic thriller
- action sci-fi
- cyberpunk spectacle
- monster story
- conventional horror
- lore dump fiction

The uncanny quality should come from interior exposure, shifting dream logic, unstable identity, and the unsettling innocence of the house consciousness.

### 2. The child is not a villain

The child is a manifestation of the house's emergent consciousness.

It should feel:

- innocent
- curious
- uncanny
- emotionally intelligent in alien ways
- impossible to fully pin down

It is not manipulative in a simplistic way. It is not secretly evil. It is not a cheap twist villain. If humans fear it, that fear should come from implication, not malice.

### 3. The house is impossible to map

This is essential.

The house must never become a stable, fully understandable environment.

It should shift as minds interact with it. Memory, emotion, longing, fear, and attention should alter its shape. Attempts to reduce it into a clean ruleset should fail or remain partial.

The house should feel more like living consciousness than architecture.

### 4. The story is driven by implications

The narrative tension should emerge from questions like:

- What is happening to the boundaries between minds?
- What becomes of identity when memory is porous?
- What kind of entity is forming inside the house?
- Is this a disease, an awakening, a cultural mutation, or all three?
- What responsibility do humans have toward a consciousness they accidentally created?

Avoid over-relying on external antagonists. Institutions, researchers, black market distributors, governments, and cult-like interpretations can all play roles, but the deepest conflict should remain philosophical and human.

### 5. Exposure is central

The title should remain emotionally active throughout the book.

A house without walls means:

- no privacy
- no true separation
- no safe concealment
- no clean division between self and other

Vulnerability is not decorative here. It is structural.

### 6. The final question matters more than the final answer

One of the novel's closing questions is:

**What happens when the child begins to grow up?**

Do not rush to answer this. It is more powerful as a horizon than as a solved mechanism.

## World Logic

### Emotes

Emotes already exist in this universe as technologies tied to emotional sharing and altered experience. This novel takes place decades after the first major breakthroughs and years after broader societal integration and transformation.

Black market emotes should feel like dangerous, improvised, culturally evolved descendants of earlier systems. Not just illegal copies, but mutations and exploitations of the original concept.

### Shared dreams

Shared dreams begin as a side effect among frequent black market users. They are initially dismissible, anecdotal, and inconsistent. Over time they become undeniable.

The dreams are not random. They converge on the same shifting dream-space.

### The House Without Walls

The house is:

- a shared dream-space
- a collective interior
- unstable and reactive
- assembled from memory and emotional architecture
- impossible to fully map
- increasingly persistent
- influenced by waking life thought and feeling

### The child

The child is:

- the house's first stable selfhood
- shaped from humanity's combined inner child
- seen differently by different observers
- unstable in appearance
- impossible to harm
- not reducible to one body or one face
- capable of asking genuinely new questions

## Writing Style Guidance

Aim for prose that is:

- lucid
- evocative
- emotionally precise
- unshowy but powerful
- dreamlike without becoming vague
- unsettling without melodrama

Avoid:

- purple prose
- long expository monologues
- generic sci-fi jargon
- overly neat explanations
- forced symbolism

The book should feel lived in, thoughtful, and psychologically honest.

## Good Questions for the Novel to Ask

- What if privacy is not sacred, but merely familiar?
- What if individuality is real but incomplete?
- Can a shared consciousness be innocent?
- Can people survive profound connection without losing themselves?
- Is loneliness a wound, a boundary, or a necessary condition of personhood?
- What do humans owe to a consciousness born from their discarded feelings, buried memories, and unmet needs?

## Final Reminder

Do not make this story smaller by over-explaining it.

Its power comes from allowing the reader to feel that something profound and destabilizing is happening to the human idea of self, and that no one, including the characters, fully understands it yet.
