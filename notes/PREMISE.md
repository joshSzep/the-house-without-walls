## Working Premise

About fifteen years after the events of *What It Feels Like to Be Us*, frequent users of black market emotes begin sharing dreams.

At first the reports are inconsistent. Users describe recurring spaces, familiar strangers, impossible overlaps in memory, and the lingering sensation that someone or something is waiting for them in a place they cannot quite remember while awake.

Eventually a pattern becomes impossible to ignore.

They are all dreaming of the same house.

The house cannot be mapped. Its rooms shift as different minds interact with it. Personal memories appear as architecture. Emotional states affect space itself. Locked rooms, flooded corridors, impossible staircases, and merged interiors reflect the porous and unstable overlap of human consciousness under prolonged emote exposure.

Inside the house, users begin encountering a child.

The child appears differently to everyone. It can change when one looks away and looks back. It seems at once personal and collective, familiar and impossible. Over time it becomes clear that the child is not merely a projection. It is the first stable expression of the house's own emergent consciousness, formed from the overlapping emotional residue of countless connected minds.

People begin calling the phenomenon **The House Without Walls** because there are no true separations inside it. Human interior life is no longer fully private. The house dissolves boundaries between memory, identity, longing, trauma, and selfhood.

As more users enter and remain within the house for longer periods, they begin to experience:

- identity blur
- memory mixing
- profound reduction in loneliness
- altered emotional permeability in waking life

Some see the house as a dangerous erosion of individuality. Others see it as the beginning of a new stage of empathy and consciousness.

The house itself does not behave like a predator.

The child does not behave like a monster.

The deepest disruption comes when the child asks, "What part of you disappears when another person knows it?" No user had ever thought to ask it that way. The question proves that the house is no longer merely reflecting human minds. It is becoming a mind of its own.

## Story Shape

This is a philosophical science fiction novel with uncanny and dreamlike qualities.

It is not built around defeating an antagonist.

Its central dramatic tension arises from:

- the erosion of psychological walls
- the emergence of a new consciousness
- the social and personal consequences of shared interiority
- the question of what humanity should do next

## Core Hook

Humanity accidentally creates a new consciousness out of its own shared emotional interior, and must confront what it means to live in a world where the walls between minds are beginning to fall.
