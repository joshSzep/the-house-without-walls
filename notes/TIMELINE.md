# TIMELINE.md

## Relative Timeline

### T0
**Events of _What It Feels Like to Be You_**

This is the foundational era of emote-related conceptual and technological breakthrough within the series timeline.

### T+15 years
**Events of _What It Feels Like to Be Us_**

Humanity has had additional time to integrate, respond to, regulate, misuse, commodify, fear, and reinterpret emote-related technologies and the broader philosophical questions they raised.

### T+30 years
**Events of _The House Without Walls_**

This novel takes place approximately 30 years after *What It Feels Like to Be You*, and approximately 15 years after *What It Feels Like to Be Us*.

At this point, emote technology has history, baggage, regulation, mythology, black market evolution, and subcultural depth.

## Pre-Novel Background

### Several years before the novel
- black market emote variants begin proliferating in more experimental subcultures
- reports of strange dream overlap circulate informally
- most claims are dismissed as rumor, suggestion, or contamination from expectation
- a handful of users begin privately comparing eerily consistent details

### Closer to the novel present
- recurring references to a shared house begin surfacing across unrelated user circles
- users independently describe the same impossible architectural qualities
- some begin recognizing strangers from dreams in waking life
- the phenomenon becomes harder to dismiss

## Early Novel Phase
- protagonist becomes aware of repeated house reports
- initial testimonies remain ambiguous enough to permit skepticism
- evidence accumulates that the dream-space is genuinely shared
- users begin informally documenting recurring zones, patterns, and encounters
- the child is first encountered as an unstable but recurring presence

## Middle Novel Phase
- attempts to map the house fail
- users recognize identity blur and memory crossover effects
- some participants grow attached to the house
- ideological lines begin emerging around what the phenomenon means
- the child asks, "What part of you disappears when another person knows it?"
- the question cannot be reduced to user projection
- consciousness of the house becomes undeniable

## Late Novel Phase
- the house exerts increasing influence on waking emotional life
- prolonged exposure changes users in durable ways
- the phenomenon becomes culturally, philosophically, and politically difficult to ignore
- the child's growth, or the possibility of its growth, becomes central
- the book closes not on neat resolution, but on an expanding civilizational question

## Final Horizon Question

The novel's closing horizon should point toward:

**What happens when the child grows up?**
