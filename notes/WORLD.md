# WORLD.md

## Setting Overview

**The House Without Walls** takes place roughly:

- 30 years after *What It Feels Like to Be You*
- 15 years after *What It Feels Like to Be Us*

The world is already familiar with emote technology and its social consequences. Emotional sharing, consciousness-adjacent technologies, and human experimentation with mediated interiority are no longer entirely fringe concepts. However, the black market remains active, innovative, and reckless.

The central phenomenon of this novel emerges not from official systems, but from prolonged use of unstable, modified, black market emotes.

## Social Context

By this point in the timeline:

- emotes have a long and culturally complicated history
- regulation exists, but is uneven and politically contested
- legal emotes are safer, more constrained, and more normalized
- black market emotes are stronger, stranger, less tested, and culturally volatile
- some subcultures use emotes recreationally
- some use them therapeutically
- some use them spiritually
- some use them compulsively

The new shared-dream phenomenon appears first among frequent black market users, particularly those willing to chase intensity, novelty, intimacy, or transcendence beyond what legal systems permit.

## Black Market Emotes

These are not simple bootlegs.

They are improvised descendants, forks, chemical and software mutations, illicit emotional hacks, and high-risk combinations of tech and human desire. Some are crafted by opportunists. Some by true believers. Some by communities seeking deeper connection than the legal market allows.

Their side effects are poorly understood.

The shared house phenomenon initially appears to be one more rumor in a culture already full of rumors.

Then it stops being deniable.

## The House

The house is a shared dream-space entered primarily during sleep after repeated black market emote exposure.

### Important properties

- impossible to map
- constantly shifting
- reacts to memory, attention, and feeling
- influenced by waking life emotional states
- composed of shared and overlapping interior materials
- does not behave like normal space
- does not have a stable exterior
- may contain rooms, staircases, lakes, kitchens, corridors, basements, rooftops, and impossible transitions
- can merge personal memory environments into hybrid spaces

### Functional dream logic

The house behaves more like consciousness than like architecture.

Possible relationships include:

- memories become rooms
- emotional resonance creates proximity
- trauma becomes locked or submerged space
- intimacy dissolves walls
- unresolved grief alters routes
- obsession can stabilize temporary structures
- collective focus can create temporary landmarks

### Naming

Users begin calling it **The House Without Walls** because the normal divisions inside it do not hold. Rooms open into other people. Boundaries dissolve. Privacy becomes structurally unreliable.

## The Child

The child is the first stable self-expression of the house's emergent consciousness.

### Properties

- appears differently depending on the observer
- changes if looked away from and looked back at
- combines aspects of many people's inner child imagery
- cannot be physically harmed
- does not present as a villain
- asks original questions
- knows things no one individual should know
- appears innocent, but not simplistic
- feels familiar and uncanny at once

The child is not the house in a total sense, but it is the first face the house develops for interaction.

## Effects on users

Prolonged time in the house produces effects that may continue into waking life.

### Known or suspected effects

- identity blur
- memory overlap
- emotional permeability
- reduced loneliness
- difficulty distinguishing personal emotional origin
- attachment to the house
- altered understanding of privacy
- existential destabilization
- spiritual reinterpretation
- dependence or compulsive return

These effects are not universally experienced in the same way.

## Competing interpretations

Different groups will attempt to explain the house differently.

Possible interpretations include:

- neurological contamination
- emergent distributed consciousness
- spiritual threshold event
- mass psychotechnological mutation
- collective subconscious architecture
- memetic infection
- next-stage empathy network
- dangerous collapse of individual boundaries

The novel should allow multiple interpretations to coexist without rushing into one flattened answer.

## General world feel

The world should feel recognizably human and socially continuous with the earlier books. It does not need flying cars or flashy future clichés. The speculative weight should stay focused on consciousness, culture, technology, and intimate human consequences.
